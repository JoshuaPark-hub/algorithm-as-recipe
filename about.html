About the Project

Algorithm as Recipe is a web-based project that reinterprets digital algorithms as culinary recipes. While algorithms are often perceived as abstract and invisible systems, this project translates them into readable, step-by-step instructions to make their structure visible.

Recipes and algorithms share a fundamental similarity: both are sets of instructions designed to transform inputs into outcomes. A recipe transforms ingredients into a dish. An algorithm transforms data into decisions. Through this analogy, the project reframes computational processes in a familiar and accessible format.

Conceptual Framework

The project is grounded in a structural comparison between culinary recipes and computational algorithms.

Recipe Component Algorithm Equivalent
Ingredients Data
Steps Code / Instructions
Kitchen Platform Infrastructure
Cook Automated System
Dish User Experience

By adopting the familiar format of a cookbook, the project emphasizes how algorithmic systems are structured, repeatable, and operational rather than mysterious.

Methodology

Each algorithm was developed through research into documented platform behaviors and personalization systems. The process involved analyzing platform logic, abstracting it into simplified steps, and rewriting it into a procedural “recipe” format.

The interface design intentionally mimics cookbook layouts and index structures. The grid system, card components, and neutral palette reinforce clarity and systematic organization.

Critical Reflection

Algorithms operate as invisible infrastructures shaping social, cultural, and economic interactions. By presenting them as recipes, this project questions their perceived neutrality and transparency.

Who writes the recipe? Who benefits from the final dish? Who becomes the ingredient?

Algorithm as Recipe invites viewers to reconsider their role within digital platforms: Are we cooks following instructions, or are we the ingredients being processed?
